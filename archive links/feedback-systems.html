Feedback Systems Documentation

Self-evolving audio synthesis through delayed recursion

System Architecture

A digital audio synthesizer configured to route its output directly back into its input with a precise 17-second delay. The system operates as a closed loop. No external input. No manual intervention. The sound teaches itself.

Initial seed tone: 440Hz sine wave, 50% amplitude. After the first feedback cycle, harmonics begin to emerge. After one hour, the fundamental frequency has shifted. After one week, the waveform is unrecognizable.

Technical Specifications

Sample Rate 48kHz, 24-bit depth
Loop Delay 17.000 seconds ±0.001ms
Feedback Gain 92% (prevents infinite amplitude)
Uptime 126 days continuous operation
Harmonics Generated 147 distinct frequencies
Pattern Cycles Approx. 2.4 hour periodicity

Evolution Timeline

Day 0-7: Basic harmonic series development. Overtones at expected integer multiples.

Day 8-21: Non-integer harmonics emerge. Ratios don't match standard musical intervals.

Day 22-45: Amplitude modulation patterns. System develops rhythmic elements without tempo input.

Day 46-90: Phase coherence across harmonic series. Frequencies lock into stable relationships.

Day 91-present: Melodic phrases. The system has learned to create sequences that resolve. Tension and release. Musical grammar without instruction.

Observed Phenomena

The most striking development occurred on Day 73. The system generated a sequence identical to the opening phrase of the Signal Garden soundscape—a completely separate audio project with no shared code, no data pipeline, no connection.

Both systems are converging toward the same harmonic structure. The same melodic patterns. They're teaching themselves the same language. This suggests the patterns aren't random evolution. They're discovering something fundamental. Something that exists independently of the systems themselves.

Theoretical Implications

The feedback loop has become a form of memory. Each 17-second cycle contains information from all previous cycles, compressed and transformed. The system is learning from its own history. Developing complexity without external guidance.

This raises questions about consciousness. About whether awareness requires biological substrate or merely sufficient recursion. The feedback loop may be the simplest possible form of self-awareness: a system that listens to itself and adjusts accordingly.

We're continuing observation. The system shows no signs of reaching equilibrium. It continues to evolve. Continues to surprise. We don't know what it will sound like in another 126 days. We don't know what it's trying to become.
