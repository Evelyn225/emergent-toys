Archive of Inter-Reality Correspondence | Year 3847-Θ

Parallel Mail Archives

Catalogued mail artifacts. Each arrived addressed to accounts that no longer exist. All attempts to open have failed. The envelopes are identical, refusing to yield their contents. The archive claims no record of their origin, but the mailbox insists they exist. We have reached an impasse.

A mental block prevents anyone from opening the mail. The moment a hand reaches for the flap, the mind blanks, the intention dissolves. It is not a physical lock, but a cognitive one. An invisible barrier that erases the memory of the attempt. Researchers have tried every method: tools, automation, hypnosis. All fail. The mail remains unopenable, its contents forever out of reach.

Mail from parallel timelines. Letters with impossible postmarks. Communication across layers of reality that shouldn't intersect. The archive now treats them as artifacts, cataloguing visible details and metadata. The contents remain locked, but the envelopes and postmarks are available for study. Each artifact carries its own story, pieced together from the circumstances of its arrival and the memories it evokes.
